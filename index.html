South African Public Holidays 2025 / 2026

This website serves as a simple, clear, and accessible resource for anyone needing information about South African public holidays for these two years.

New Year's Day - January 1

Description

New Year's Day, also simply called New Year, is observed on 1 January, the first day of the year on the modern Gregorian calendar as well as the Julian calendar.

Dress Code

  • Men: Festive casual wear, button-up shirt with slacks. Example: A crisp white shirt with navy chinos and loafers.
  • Women: Cocktail dress or festive blouse with skirt/pants. Example: A sparkly top with black pants or a knee-length party dress.
  • Children: Comfortable festive wear. Example: Nice jeans with a collared shirt for boys, or a colorful dress for girls.

Gift Ideas

  • Personalized calendar
  • Goal-setting journal
  • Fitness tracker
  • Inspirational book
  • Gourmet food hamper

Human Rights Day - March 21

Description

Human Rights Day in South Africa is historically linked with 21 March 1960, and the events of Sharpeville. On that day 69 people died and 180 were wounded.

Dress Code

  • Men: Smart casual with national colors. Example: Khaki trousers with a shirt in colors of the South African flag.
  • Women: Comfortable attire with South African-inspired accessories. Example: A sundress with beaded jewelry in national colors.
  • Children: Casual clothes with bright colors. Example: T-shirts with South African wildlife prints.

Gift Ideas

  • Books on South African history
  • Locally made crafts
  • Donation to a human rights organization
  • South African flag-themed items
  • Educational games about rights and equality

Good Friday - April 18

Description

Good Friday is a Christian holiday commemorating the crucifixion of Jesus and his death at Calvary. It is observed during Holy Week as part of the Paschal Triduum.

Dress Code

  • Men: Formal church attire. Example: A suit or dress pants with a button-up shirt and tie.
  • Women: Modest church dress. Example: A knee-length dress or skirt with a blouse.
  • Children: Smart attire. Example: A collared shirt with slacks for boys, or a dress for girls.

Gift Ideas

  • Bible or religious books
  • Handmade cross or religious symbols
  • Prayer beads
  • Religious artwork
  • Charity donation in someone's name

Easter Monday - April 21

Description

Easter Monday is a Christian holiday celebrated the day after Easter Sunday. It is a public holiday in South Africa and a time for family gatherings and celebrations.

Dress Code

  • Men: Casual comfortable wear. Example: Jeans with a polo shirt.
  • Women: Casual spring attire. Example: A light dress or skirt and top.
  • Children: Playful clothes. Example: Comfortable jeans or shorts with a T-shirt.

Gift Ideas

  • Chocolate eggs
  • Spring-themed decorations
  • Board games for family fun
  • Baking kits for homemade treats
  • Plant seeds or gardening kits

Freedom Day - April 27

Description

Freedom Day is a South African public holiday celebrated on 27 April. It celebrates freedom and commemorates the first post-apartheid elections held on that day in 1994.

Dress Code

  • Men: National colors attire. Example: A casual shirt in the colors of the South African flag with jeans.
  • Women: Comfortable attire with African print. Example: A skirt or dress with South African fabric patterns.
  • Children: Casual attire with fun prints. Example: T-shirts with African animal designs.

Gift Ideas

  • Historical documentaries or books
  • South African cultural items
  • Donation to community organizations
  • Freedom-themed artwork
  • South African music collections

Workers' Day - May 1

Description

Workers' Day, also known as Labour Day, is celebrated on 1 May to honour workers and the contribution they make to society. It is a day to celebrate workers' rights and freedoms.

Dress Code

  • Men: Comfortable and casual. Example: Denim jeans with a t-shirt or polo shirt.
  • Women: Casual and relaxed. Example: A comfortable dress or jeans with a blouse.
  • Children: Playful and fun. Example: Shorts and a t-shirt.

Gift Ideas

  • Inspirational books on leadership
  • Gift cards for relaxation (e.g., spa, restaurant)
  • Work-life balance journals
  • Artisanal snacks and beverages
  • Eco-friendly office supplies

Youth Day - June 16

Description

Youth Day in South Africa commemorates the Soweto Uprising on June 16, 1976, where thousands of black students protested against the imposition of Afrikaans as the medium of instruction.

Dress Code

  • Men: Casual and colorful attire. Example: Jeans with a vibrant T-shirt.
  • Women: Youthful and energetic. Example: Bright dress or top with jeans.
  • Children: Playful and practical. Example: T-shirt and shorts with sneakers.

Gift Ideas

  • Educational books or tools
  • Sports equipment
  • Art supplies for creativity
  • Music or movie vouchers
  • Donation to youth programs

National Women's Day - August 9

Description

National Women's Day commemorates the 1956 march of approximately 20,000 women to the Union Buildings in Pretoria to protest against the pass laws.

Dress Code

  • Men: Supportive and respectful attire. Example: Wearing a purple ribbon in solidarity.
  • Women: Wear purple, symbolizing justice and dignity. Example: A purple blouse or dress.
  • Children: Casual clothing with a touch of purple. Example: A purple T-shirt.

Gift Ideas

  • Books by female authors
  • Donation to women's shelters
  • Self-care packages
  • Jewelry supporting women's causes
  • Subscription to a women's magazine

Heritage Day - September 24

Description

Heritage Day is a South African public holiday celebrated on 24 September. On this day, South Africans across the spectrum are encouraged to celebrate their culture and the diversity of their beliefs and traditions.

Dress Code

  • Men: Traditional attire or smart casual. Example: A Madiba shirt or traditional attire.
  • Women: Traditional attire or smart casual. Example: Traditional Zulu dress or colorful skirt and blouse.
  • Children: Traditional attire or casual wear. Example: Beaded accessories with everyday clothing.

Gift Ideas

  • South African cookbooks
  • Handmade crafts
  • Traditional music albums
  • Cultural artifacts
  • Donations to cultural preservation societies

Day of Reconciliation - December 16

Description

The Day of Reconciliation is a public holiday in South Africa held annually on 16 December. The holiday came into effect in 1995 after the end of apartheid, with the intention of fostering reconciliation and national unity.

Dress Code

  • Men: Smart-casual with a peace symbol. Example: A shirt with a peace logo and jeans.
  • Women: Comfortable and peace-themed. Example: A white dress or outfit symbolizing peace.
  • Children: Casual with symbols of unity. Example: T-shirts with a rainbow or peace sign.

Gift Ideas

  • Books on peace and unity
  • Peace-themed accessories
  • Donations to reconciliation projects
  • Art representing diversity and unity
  • Charitable giving in the name of peace

Christmas Day is an annual festival commemorating the birth of Jesus Christ, observed primarily on December 25 as a religious and cultural celebration among billions of people around the world.

Day of Goodwill - December 26

Description

The Day of Goodwill, celebrated on December 26, encourages South Africans to extend goodwill to others, especially the less fortunate, by sharing and spreading happiness.

Dress Code

  • Men: Casual and comfortable. Example: Jeans with a T-shirt or polo shirt.
  • Women: Relaxed and comfortable. Example: Casual dress or shorts with a top.
  • Children: Playful attire. Example: Comfortable clothes suitable for outdoor activities.

Gift Ideas

  • Homemade food or baked goods
  • Volunteer experiences
  • Books or games promoting kindness
  • Community service vouchers

About South African Public Holidays

When a public holiday falls on a Sunday, the following Monday becomes a public holiday. This is determined by the Public Holidays Act (Act No 36 of 1994).

On these days, Sunday shopping hours usually apply, except for Christmas and Easter when most shops will be closed.

The dates for Good Friday and Easter Sunday are determined according to the ecclesiastical moon and vary each year, falling between late March and late April.
